The Problem
Rent and utilities are the two biggest bills —
and the most expensive to pay.
🧾

Renters Pay to Pay

Paying rent or a utility bill online carries a $2–$3.95 convenience fee per payment (or 2.5–3.5% on card). On rent + utilities every month, that's a recurring tax on the largest checks a household writes.

$2–$3.95
convenience fee per bill payment
🏦

Checking Earns ~0%

The ~$2,000 a household parks in checking to cover rent + utilities earns essentially nothing — banks hold the float and keep the T-bill income for themselves.

~0%
earned on rent/utility float in checking
🏢

Operators Pay & Get Nothing

Apartment operators and utilities pay processing costs and SaaS fees to collect, sit on large parked balances between cycles, and earn nothing on that float. Bilt processes $45B+/yr in rent — proof the volume is enormous.

Business Model
Free to the renter. $2 flat to the operator.

How VaultaPay Makes Money

  • Transaction fees — $2 flat per bill payment, paid by the operator/utility. ~50% of the $3.95 incumbents charge, and below Bilt's 0.6–0.9% on a typical rent.
  • Net float — balances sit in T-bills; VaultaPay keeps a sleeve and pays the rest back as Vaulta Rewards to both sides.
  • Free to the renter, free P2P — zero consumer fees drive adoption; the operator is the paying customer.
